Chicken Stir Fry

Description

Chicken stir fry is a dish made from chicken that is quickly fried with various vegetables, such as peas, carrots, bell peppers, and onions. The dish is usually seasoned with soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and other spices. Chicken stir fry is a popular dish in many countries, including China, Thailand, and the United States.

This quick and easy chicken stir fry recipe is sure to become a mainstay in your weeknight dinner rotation.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, minced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1/2 cup snow peas
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp cornstarch

Instructions

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the chicken, soy sauce, cornstarch, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat the chicken evenly.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned, about 5 minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  3. Add the bell pepper, onion, garlic, and ginger to the skillet. Cook for 2-3 minutes, until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Add the broccoli and snow peas to the skillet. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the chicken broth, oyster sauce, soy sauce, and cornstarch. Pour the sauce over the vegetables in the skillet.
  6. Return the chicken to the skillet and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the sauce has thickened and the chicken is cooked through.
  7. Serve the chicken stir fry over rice or noodles.
